Help

Common questions, answered plainly.

How is confidentiality handled?

An NDA can be signed before sensitive records change hands, and redacted samples are welcome whenever the matter allows them. The transfer method and document-retention instructions are confirmed in writing before files are requested.

Please do not attach bank statements or other sensitive records to an initial inquiry.

The practice prepares data only, so it never appears in your case as an expert or opinion-giver, and your engagement's independence stays intact.

What files can you work from?

Bank and credit-card statements as PDFs, whether digitally generated or scanned. Imperfect scans, odd layouts, and older statements can be reviewed. Brokerage statements and unfamiliar formats require a sample review before they are included in a quote.

What exactly comes back?

Four things: a transaction schedule (clean CSV or Excel, one row per transaction), Quicken/QIF import files when the workflow uses them, an exception schedule listing every entry that could not be resolved with certainty, and a reconciliation summary documenting the checks performed against the statements.

Nothing is silently guessed. If an item is uncertain, it is flagged, not buried.

How fast is turnaround?

Small pilot batches are typically returned within one business day. Larger projects receive a written schedule before work begins. Rush availability depends on the batch size and is confirmed in the quote.

How does the pilot engagement work?

Every new relationship starts with a small pilot covering up to three hours of preparation. The pilot is capped at ninety dollars. A redacted sample can be used when appropriate, and the larger project is quoted only after the pilot establishes the file quality and expected workload.

How is ongoing work priced?

Ongoing work is quoted by batch after a sample review. The quote considers statement volume, scan quality, requested output, and deadline. Large projects receive volume pricing, and a written price and schedule always precede the work.

What does the practice not do?

Accounting opinions, legal conclusions, tax advice, expert testimony, and analysis of any kind. Those remain entirely with your team. The service ends where judgment begins: you receive accurate, reconciled, well-documented data and take it from there.

How is the work handled?

Engagements remain under the direct control of Troppe Financial Data Services. Work is not passed to offshore data-entry shops or anonymous labor pools, and questions are handled directly through the practice.
